1. SEVERITY LEVELS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

P0 — CRITICAL
  Definition:  Full production outage OR security breach affecting customer data.
  Examples:    API gateway down, database unreachable, confirmed data exfiltration
  Response SLA: Acknowledge within 15 minutes, mitigate within 1 hour
  Notification: Page on-call engineer immediately. Notify VP Eng + CEO within 30 min.

P1 — HIGH
  Definition:  Major functionality degraded; significant business impact.
  Examples:    CI/CD pipeline broken, VPN down for >10 users, phishing campaign
  Response SLA: Acknowledge within 30 minutes, resolve within 4 hours
  Notification: Alert on-call + IT Manager. Notify affected teams via Slack #incidents.

P2 — MEDIUM
  Definition:  Non-critical service impaired; moderate business impact.
  Examples:    SSO errors for a subset of users, hardware failure for one employee,
               analytics dashboard delayed
  Response SLA: Acknowledge within 2 hours, resolve within 1 business day
  Notification: Create a ticket in helpdesk.acmecorp.com. Notify affected users.

P3 — LOW
  Definition:  Minor issue; minimal business impact.
  Examples:    Printer offline, individual laptop issue, permissions tweak needed
  Response SLA: Acknowledge within 8 business hours, resolve within 3 business days
  Notification: Create a ticket. No immediate page required.

2. INCIDENT RESPONSE PROCEDURE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Step 1 — DETECT & REPORT
  Anyone can open an incident:
  - IT Helpdesk portal: helpdesk.acmecorp.com → "New Incident"
  - Slack command:      /incident create <title> in #incidents channel
  - Email:              incidents@acmecorp.com (auto-creates P2 ticket)
  - Phone (P0/P1 only): +1-415-555-0100 (24/7 on-call line)

Step 2 — TRIAGE & ASSIGN
  On-call engineer reviews the report within the SLA window:
  1. Confirm severity (P0–P3)
  2. Assign to the most appropriate team member
  3. Set incident status to "In Progress" in the ticketing system
  4. Post initial update to #incidents Slack channel

Step 3 — COMMUNICATE
  For P0/P1, post updates to #incidents every 30 minutes until resolved.
  Template:
    [INC-XXX] Status Update — <time>
    Current status: <what is happening now>
    Impact: <who/what is affected>
    Next action: <what we are doing in the next 30 min>
    ETA to resolution: <estimate or "TBD">

Step 4 — MITIGATE & RESOLVE
  Priority is to restore service, not find root cause. Common mitigations:
  - Rollback: Deploy the previous stable version
  - Feature flag: Disable the affected feature
  - Traffic shift: Route traffic away from affected region/service
  - Restart: Restart the affected service/pod/container

Step 5 — POST-INCIDENT REVIEW (PIR)
  Required for all P0 incidents. Recommended for P1.
  - PIR document due within 5 business days of resolution
  - Template: drive.acmecorp.com/it/pir-template
  - Review meeting required for P0 within 48 hours
