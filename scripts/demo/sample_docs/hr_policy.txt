1. PAID TIME OFF (PTO)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1.1 Annual Entitlement
Employees accrue PTO based on tenure:
  - 0–2 years:      20 days / year (1.67 days per month)
  - 2–5 years:      25 days / year (2.08 days per month)
  - 5+ years:       30 days / year (2.50 days per month)

Accrual begins on the first day of employment. New hires may use accrued PTO
after completing 60 days of continuous employment.

1.2 PTO Requests
Requests must be submitted in Workday at least:
  - 2 business days advance notice for 1–2 day absences
  - 5 business days for 3–5 day absences
  - 3 weeks for absences longer than 5 days
Manager approval is required. During peak business periods (Q4 October–December),
PTO requests may be limited at manager discretion.

1.3 Rollover Policy
Up to 5 PTO days may be carried over to the following calendar year.
Days in excess of 5 are forfeited on December 31 each year.

1.4 PTO Payout on Termination
Accrued, unused PTO is paid out upon voluntary resignation or termination,
up to a maximum of 15 days. PTO is not paid out for employees terminated
for cause.

2. SICK LEAVE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Employees receive 10 sick days per year, separate from PTO. Sick days do not
accrue — the full 10 days are available on January 1 each year (or on the
hire date for new employees, pro-rated). Unused sick days do not carry over
and are not paid out on termination.

Sick leave may be used for:
  - Personal illness or injury
  - Medical appointments
  - Care of an immediate family member (spouse, child, parent)

3. REMOTE WORK POLICY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

3.1 Standard Remote Work
All employees may work remotely up to 2 days per week unless designated
"Remote" in their employment agreement. Employees at Level 6 (Staff / Senior
Manager) and above may work remotely up to 4 days per week.

3.2 Fully Remote Employees
Employees whose employment contract designates "Remote" as their primary
location are not required to come to an office. They are expected to travel
to headquarters for:
  - Quarterly all-hands meetings
  - Annual performance calibration
  - Team offsites (as directed by their manager)

3.3 Core Hours
Regardless of location, all employees are expected to be available during
core hours: 10:00 AM – 3:00 PM in their local timezone. Meetings outside
this window require advance agreement from all participants.

4. BENEFITS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

4.1 Health Insurance
Acme offers three medical plan tiers through Aetna:
  - HMO Bronze:   Employee pays $0/month. $3,000 individual deductible.
  - PPO Silver:   Employee pays $150/month. $1,500 individual deductible.
  - PPO Gold:     Employee pays $350/month. $500 individual deductible.
Dental (MetLife) and Vision (VSP) are provided at no cost to the employee.
Dependents may be added to all plans; employee contributions vary by tier and
number of dependents (see benefits.acmecorp.com for rates).

4.2 401(k) Retirement Plan
Acme matches 100% of employee contributions up to 4% of base salary.
Vesting schedule: 25% per year, fully vested after 4 years.
Employees may contribute up to IRS limits ($23,500 in 2026 for under-50).

4.3 Equity
All full-time employees receive RSU grants. Vesting is over 4 years with a
1-year cliff (25% vest at 12 months, 1/48 per month thereafter). Grant sizes
vary by level; see your offer letter or speak to your manager.

4.4 Additional Benefits
  - $150/month remote work stipend (fully remote employees)
  - $1,500/year professional development budget
  - $100/month wellness benefit (gym, meditation apps, etc.)
  - 12 weeks fully paid parental leave (primary caregiver)
  - 4 weeks fully paid parental leave (secondary caregiver)
  - Employee Assistance Program (EAP): free confidential counselling

5. PERFORMANCE REVIEWS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

5.1 Annual Review Cycle
  - Mid-year check-in:   June (informal; no rating or compensation change)
  - Annual review:       November–December (formal; rating + compensation review)

5.2 Rating Scale
  1 — Does Not Meet Expectations
  2 — Partially Meets Expectations
  3 — Meets Expectations (target for most employees)
  4 — Exceeds Expectations
  5 — Outstanding

5.3 Merit Increases
Merit increases (effective February 1) are determined by rating:
  Rating 3: 2–4%    Rating 4: 4–7%    Rating 5: 7–12%
  Ratings 1 or 2: no merit increase; Performance Improvement Plan initiated.

6. LEAVES OF ABSENCE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

  - Parental Leave:     12 weeks paid (primary) / 4 weeks paid (secondary)
  - Medical Leave:      Up to 12 weeks per FMLA; first 4 weeks fully paid,
                        remaining 8 weeks at 60% via short-term disability
  - Bereavement:        5 days for immediate family; 2 days for extended family
  - Jury Duty:          Full pay for up to 10 business days
